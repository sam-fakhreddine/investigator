# Glossary — Sure Good Foods — Pitch Intelligence for Website Redesign

Quick definitions of key terms and concepts referenced in this investigation.

---

## International Commodity Trader

A firm that buys and sells physical food commodities across borders on behalf of producers and buyers, without owning production infrastructure. SGF earns margin on sourcing, logistics coordination, and market access rather than on manufacturing or branding.

## GFSI Certification

Global Food Safety Initiative — an industry-driven body that benchmarks food safety management standards (SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000, IFS); many major retailers and foodservice distributors require GFSI certification from suppliers as a prerequisite and expect to verify it on the supplier's website.

## Brochure Site

A website structured as a static informational document with four to five top-level pages and no depth of content within sections; appropriate for companies that acquire customers exclusively through referrals and relationships, but limits inbound discovery and self-service buyer qualification.

## Canonical URL

An HTML directive (rel=canonical) or HTTP redirect (301) that tells search engines which version of a duplicate or near-duplicate page is authoritative; when absent from suregoodfoods.com, Google indexes both /services and /services-2 as competing pages, diluting ranking signals.

## Orphaned Page

A web page that is published and accessible via URL but not linked from any navigation, sitemap, or internal link — often a byproduct of CMS page duplication; on suregoodfoods.com, /services-2 and /products-3 are live in Google's index despite being unlinked from the primary navigation.

## B2B Lead Qualification Form

A structured web form capturing buyer intent signals beyond a name and email — company name, buyer role, product category, volume requirement, destination market — used to route inbound inquiries to the correct trader and pre-qualify leads before first contact.

## Structured Data Markup (Schema.org)

Machine-readable JSON-LD annotations in HTML that allow search engines to understand page content type (Organization, Product, LocalBusiness) and display rich results in SERPs; absence from suregoodfoods.com means the site cannot qualify for enhanced SERP real estate.

## Google Business Profile (GBP)

Google's free business listing appearing in branded and local search results; an unclaimed or unoptimized GBP leaves SERP real estate to third-party directory sites and legitimacy-checking services, which damages first impressions for non-referral procurement buyers.

## Argyle Foods Group

An Australian vertically integrated beef producer offering certified grassfed, GMO-free, antibiotic-free, and HGP-free beef. SGF's November 2023 commercial partnership with Argyle is its most visible move into premium certified protein supply chains — a differentiator not mentioned on the current website.

## HGP-Free

Hormone Growth Promotant-free — a product certification that beef was raised without synthetic growth hormones; a key requirement for EU market access and premium retail buyers in North America and Australia, and part of the Argyle Foods partnership value proposition.

## Top-of-Funnel Email Capture

A mechanism (newsletter signup, downloadable sourcing guide, market alert subscription) that collects email addresses from site visitors who are researching but not yet ready to inquire; entirely absent on suregoodfoods.com, meaning the site cannot nurture warm buyers before they reach out to sales.

## Sure-Good House Brand

The historical retail brand of the Forgan family meat-packing business in Winnipeg, Manitoba, founded by WWII veteran Jack Forgan. Co-founder David Forgan adopted this heritage name for the 2017 rebrand — a compelling origin story not surfaced anywhere on the current website.
