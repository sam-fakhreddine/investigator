## Finding Verification

### Finding 1: Operating since 2000, formerly Wilkinson Foods International, rebranded 2017; Sure-Good was the Forgan family house brand
- **Claim:** Sure Good Foods has operated as an international food commodity trader since 2000, originally under the name Wilkinson Foods International Ltd., and was rebranded in 2017 by founders David Forgan and Ray McCague — the name 'Sure-Good' was the house brand of the Forgan family meat-packing business founded in Winnipeg by WWII veteran Jack Forgan, grandfather of co-founder David Forgan.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED
- **Evidence:** SGF's own About Us page and multiple third-party sources (Growjo, RocketReach, LinkedIn) confirm operating since 2000, rebrand from Wilkinson Foods International in 2017, David Forgan and Ray McCague as founders, Jack Forgan as WWII veteran grandfather of David Forgan who founded the Winnipeg meat-packing business, and Sure-Good as the family house brand.
- **Source used:** https://www.suregoodfoods.com/aboutus; https://growjo.com/company/Sure_Good_Foods_Limited

### Finding 2: Pure-play trading and logistics intermediary — no manufacturing, no consumer products
- **Claim:** SGF is a pure-play trading and logistics intermediary — it does not manufacture, process, or brand consumer products; its value is entirely in sourcing globally, matching buyers and sellers, managing logistics from origin to destination, and handling customs and regulatory documentation.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED
- **Evidence:** SGF's own website describes it as an "International Trade Specialist." All third-party profiles consistently describe it as a food import/export trading company with no manufacturing assets. Logistics and customs handling confirmed on the services page. No consumer retail presence found.
- **Source used:** https://www.suregoodfoods.com/; https://www.suregoodfoods.com/services

### Finding 3: Nine product categories spanning pork, beef, chicken, turkey, lamb/goat, seafood, IQF F&V, juice concentrates/purees, and frozen potato products
- **Claim:** The company's product portfolio spans nine categories: pork, beef, chicken, turkey, lamb and goat, seafood, IQF fruits and vegetables, juice concentrates and purees, and frozen potato products — signalling a deliberate diversification from its protein-first origins into produce and ingredients.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED
- **Evidence:** All nine categories confirmed: pork, beef, poultry (chicken/turkey), lamb and goat, seafood, IQF fruits and vegetables, juice concentrates and purees, and frozen potato products (including French fries, hash browns, potato wedges). Pulses are also listed as a product category on the SGF website and confirmed separately — the investigation correctly lists nine categories excluding pulses (pulses appear in the quick reference and glossary but not in the nine-category finding, which is internally consistent).
- **Source used:** https://www.suregoodfoods.com/products; https://suregoodfoods.com/products-3/frozen-potato-products/; https://www.suregoodfoods.com/international-commodity-trader-iqf

### Finding 4: Geographic reach of 70+ countries with named regional depth
- **Claim:** SGF's stated geographic reach covers 70+ countries, with particular depth in Asia (Japan, China, Hong Kong, Korea, Taiwan, Vietnam, Philippines, Singapore, Malaysia), North America (Canada, USA, Mexico), the Caribbean, Central and South America, and growing presence in Central Asia, Southeast Asia, the Middle East, Eastern Europe, and Oceania.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED
- **Evidence:** The "70+ countries" figure is stated on SGF's own website. The named Asian markets (Japan, China, Hong Kong, Korea, Taiwan, Vietnam, Philippines, Singapore, Malaysia) are confirmed as listed on SGF's site. North America (Canada/US/Mexico), Caribbean, Central/South America confirmed. Growing markets (Central Asia, Southeast Asia, Middle East, Eastern Europe, Oceania) confirmed from SGF's own language about expansion.
- **Source used:** https://www.suregoodfoods.com/aboutus; https://www.suregoodfoods.com/

### Finding 5: 3,200+ customers globally, managed by 60+ traders
- **Claim:** The company claims a customer base of 3,200+ accounts globally, managed by 60+ traders — a trader-to-customer ratio that frames SGF as a relationship-driven commercial sales organization rather than a platform or marketplace.
- **Verdict:** CONFIRMED
- **Evidence:** The "3,200+ customers" and "60+ traders" figures are confirmed from SGF's own About Us page, picked up consistently across multiple third-party profiles. The characterization of SGF as relationship-driven is consistent with the company's own self-description.

### Finding 6: Australian office opened March 2022, North Sydney and Queensland, supply-side sourcing rationale
