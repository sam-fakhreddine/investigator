## Question

> What does a website designer or agency need to know about Sure Good Foods and their current digital presence to pitch and win a website redesign engagement?

---

## Context

Sure Good Foods Ltd. (SGF) is a Mississauga-based international food commodity trading company founded in 2000 (rebranded 2017) with estimated $19.6M USD in annual revenue, 3,200+ B2B customers across 70+ countries, and offices in Canada, Australia, and Dubai. Despite this operational scale, the company's digital presence is thin: its website is a brochure-grade Wix build with a 2021 copyright date, no content marketing, no trust signals, and active SEO problems including duplicate indexed URLs. The gap between who SGF is and how they present online is the core pitch opportunity. This rollup synthesizes findings from two sub-investigations — one into SGF's company profile and one into their website's technical and content weaknesses — into pitch-ready intelligence for a web design agency preparing to engage SGF on a redesign.

---

## Key Findings

- Sure Good Foods is a credentialed, 25-year-old international food trading company with 3,200+ B2B customers across 70+ countries and an estimated $19.6M USD in annual revenue — but its website presents as a minimal brochure site with a 2021 copyright date and no content depth, creating a wide gap between actual operational scale and perceived digital credibility.
- The website is built on Wix, a consumer-oriented website builder not optimized for B2B technical SEO or content scaling; this platform choice, combined with the absence of a sitemap governance process, has produced at least two duplicate or orphaned pages indexed by Google (/services-2, /products-3) with no canonical tags or 301 redirects in place.
- Title tags across suregoodfoods.com contain zero product-category or service-level keywords; all organic search visibility is limited to branded queries from buyers who already know the company; SGF is effectively invisible to procurement managers searching for 'IQF vegetable supplier Canada,' 'frozen poultry distributor,' or similar transactional B2B terms.
- No food safety certifications (GFSI, SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000) are surfaced anywhere on the website or in indexed metadata; this is a disqualifying gap for procurement buyers at major retailers or foodservice distributors who require GFSI certification as a prerequisite and expect to verify it self-service on the supplier's website.
- A ScamMinder legitimacy-check page for suregoodfoods.com appears in branded Google search results, indicating SGF has insufficient authoritative web presence (press coverage, trade publication mentions, consistent directory citations) to dominate its own branded SERP — this is an active first-impression trust liability for non-referral procurement buyers.
- No social proof elements — client logos, testimonials, partner badges, or case studies — are visible on any indexed page; competitor benchmarks AJC International and Quirch Foods surface certifications, geographic reach maps, and industry affiliations on dedicated quality and services pages — a standard that suregoodfoods.com does not meet anywhere on the site.
- The contact pathway is a generic contact form with no buyer qualification fields; B2B food distributor best practice is a structured inquiry form capturing company name, buyer role, product category, approximate volume, and destination market to route leads to the right trader and pre-qualify them before first contact.
- SGF's content marketing footprint is zero: no blog, no trade content, no whitepapers, no sourcing guides, no market alerts — the LinkedIn company page (5,262 followers) is the only content distribution channel with any active audience, and the Australian subsidiary LinkedIn page has only 67 followers.
- SGF's actual business story — 25-year operating history, Forgan family heritage brand ('Sure-Good'), 60+ traders globally, four North American export trade association memberships, a 2023 premium protein partnership with Argyle Foods Group, and offices in three continents — is compelling and entirely invisible on the current website.
- The 2023 commercial partnership with Argyle Foods Group (certified grassfed, GMO-free, antibiotic-free, HGP-free beef) signals SGF's strategic move into premium and certification-backed supply chains; this differentiator belongs on the website and currently is not mentioned anywhere in indexed site content.
- The strategic framing for a pitch is the gap between who SGF is and how they present: a 25-year, multi-continent, 3,200-customer trading firm that presents online like a startup that built a five-page site and stopped; the redesign pitch is not about aesthetics — it is about making SGF's real capabilities discoverable and credible to buyers who find them for the first time.

## Concepts & Entities

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| International Commodity Trader | A firm that buys and sells physical food commodities across borders on behalf of producers and buyers, without owning production infrastructure. SGF earns margin on sourcing, logistics coordination, and market access rather than on manufacturing or branding. |
| GFSI Certification | Global Food Safety Initiative — an industry-driven body that benchmarks food safety management standards (SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000, IFS); many major retailers and foodservice distributors require GFSI certification from suppliers as a prerequisite and expect to verify it on the supplier's website. |
| Brochure Site | A website structured as a static informational document with four to five top-level pages and no depth of content within sections; appropriate for companies that acquire customers exclusively through referrals and relationships, but limits inbound discovery and self-service buyer qualification. |
| Canonical URL | An HTML directive (rel=canonical) or HTTP redirect (301) that tells search engines which version of a duplicate or near-duplicate page is authoritative; when absent from suregoodfoods.com, Google indexes both /services and /services-2 as competing pages, diluting ranking signals. |
| Orphaned Page | A web page that is published and accessible via URL but not linked from any navigation, sitemap, or internal link — often a byproduct of CMS page duplication; on suregoodfoods.com, /services-2 and /products-3 are live in Google's index despite being unlinked from the primary navigation. |
| B2B Lead Qualification Form | A structured web form capturing buyer intent signals beyond a name and email — company name, buyer role, product category, volume requirement, destination market — used to route inbound inquiries to the correct trader and pre-qualify leads before first contact. |
| Structured Data Markup (Schema.org) | Machine-readable JSON-LD annotations in HTML that allow search engines to understand page content type (Organization, Product, LocalBusiness) and display rich results in SERPs; absence from suregoodfoods.com means the site cannot qualify for enhanced SERP real estate. |
| Google Business Profile (GBP) | Google's free business listing appearing in branded and local search results; an unclaimed or unoptimized GBP leaves SERP real estate to third-party directory sites and legitimacy-checking services, which damages first impressions for non-referral procurement buyers. |
| Argyle Foods Group | An Australian vertically integrated beef producer offering certified grassfed, GMO-free, antibiotic-free, and HGP-free beef. SGF's November 2023 commercial partnership with Argyle is its most visible move into premium certified protein supply chains — a differentiator not mentioned on the current website. |
| HGP-Free | Hormone Growth Promotant-free — a product certification that beef was raised without synthetic growth hormones; a key requirement for EU market access and premium retail buyers in North America and Australia, and part of the Argyle Foods partnership value proposition. |
| Top-of-Funnel Email Capture | A mechanism (newsletter signup, downloadable sourcing guide, market alert subscription) that collects email addresses from site visitors who are researching but not yet ready to inquire; entirely absent on suregoodfoods.com, meaning the site cannot nurture warm buyers before they reach out to sales. |
| Sure-Good House Brand | The historical retail brand of the Forgan family meat-packing business in Winnipeg, Manitoba, founded by WWII veteran Jack Forgan. Co-founder David Forgan adopted this heritage name for the 2017 rebrand — a compelling origin story not surfaced anywhere on the current website. |

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- SGF's operational scale (3,200+ customers, 70+ countries, $19.6M estimated revenue) is far larger than its digital footprint implies; a pitch that leads with this gap is compelling to leadership, but may land as criticism to someone who built or approved the current site — the framing must position the gap as a growth opportunity, not a failure.
- Fixing the duplicate page and URL taxonomy issues requires CMS discipline and likely a platform migration, but the more impactful gap — zero content marketing and zero trust signals — is a strategic and resource allocation decision that a website redesign alone cannot solve; the agency must set expectations about what a redesign delivers versus what an ongoing content program delivers.
- The absence of food safety certifications on the website may reflect certifications that are held but not surfaced online, or it may be a genuine gap in certifications — the remediation path differs completely; the pitch must probe this question without implying the client has a compliance failure.
- SGF's primary customer acquisition channel is relationship-driven (traders, trade shows, referrals), not inbound digital; the ROI case for a redesign depends entirely on whether SGF wants to add a digital inbound channel, which is a strategic choice the company has not publicly made; the pitch must help the client articulate this choice, not assume it.
- A Wix-to-modern-platform migration delivers the largest technical SEO and content scalability gains, but it also requires the client to commit to ongoing content production and maintenance — pitching the migration without a content strategy creates a technically superior but equally empty website.

## Open Questions

- Does Sure Good Foods hold any GFSI-recognized certifications (SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000) that are simply not surfaced on the website, or is this a genuine certification gap — the answer determines whether the pitch is 'show what you have' or 'advise on what to pursue'?
- What share of new customer acquisition currently comes through inbound digital channels versus trade shows, referrals, and direct trader outreach — this number determines the ROI case for digital investment and should be elicited in a discovery call?
- Who owns and manages the current Wix site internally, and what is their capacity and appetite for ongoing content production — the pitch must account for the operational reality of whoever will maintain the new site?
- Is the Google Business Profile for the Mississauga HQ claimed and actively managed, or unclaimed — this is a zero-cost quick win that the agency can offer as a pre-pitch demonstration of value?
- How has the Argyle Foods Group partnership developed since November 2023 — if it is active and generating volume, it is a premium differentiator that belongs prominently on the redesigned site?
- Does SGF have proprietary certifications (Halal, Kosher, organic, GlobalG.A.P.) that govern specific product lines or market access — these belong on the website and may be entirely absent from current indexed content?
- Who are SGF's primary competitors in the Canadian-headquartered international protein and produce trading space, and how does their digital presence compare — AJC International and Quirch Foods are US-headquartered benchmarks but Canadian-market comparisons may be more persuasive in a pitch?
