# Investigation: Sure Good Foods — Complete Business Profile for Website Design

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Status:** Complete

---

## Question

> What is Sure Good Foods' complete business profile — company history, founding story, leadership team, product portfolio with specifics, certifications held or claimed, facility details, market differentiators, brand positioning, geographic reach, industry memberships, and strategic partnerships — to inform the design and content of their new website?

---

## Context

Sure Good Foods Ltd. (SGF) is a Mississauga-based international food commodity trading company operating since 2000 under the original name Wilkinson Foods International, rebranded in 2017. The company is a pure-play trading intermediary — it does not manufacture or process food — earning margin on sourcing, logistics coordination, customs documentation, and market access across 70+ countries. This investigation synthesizes verified facts from the company's own website, trade publications, import/export records, third-party business directories, and industry event listings to produce a comprehensive reference for website designers and content writers building SGF's new digital presence.

## Key Findings

- Sure Good Foods Ltd. traces its origins to 2000 when Joe Wilkinson founded Wilkinson Foods International Ltd. in Mississauga, Ontario, envisioning an international meat trading company rich in product diversity with strong employee and management relations. In 2017, David Forgan (who had served as Director of American Sales at Wilkinson Foods) and Ray McCague (Director of Export Sales at Wilkinson Foods) rebranded the company as Sure Good Foods Ltd. The new name was drawn directly from the Forgan family heritage: David's grandfather, Jack Forgan, a World War II veteran, had established a meat-packing business in Winnipeg, Manitoba, whose house brand was 'Sure-Good.' This multi-generational food industry lineage — from a post-war Winnipeg meat-packing house to a modern international commodity trading firm — is a compelling brand story that is currently invisible on the company's website.
- The executive leadership team extends well beyond the three previously known names. Troy Warren serves as President, having previously held the position of Vice President at Maple Leaf Foods (one of Canada's largest food companies) and holding a degree from Wilfrid Laurier University. David Typer is Chief Operating Officer with 10 direct reports. Robert Kubar, CPA, CA serves as Chief Financial Officer. The regional leadership includes Michael Niblett as Managing Director of the Australian division (based in North Sydney), Jamie Martel as Managing Director for the USA (based in Atlanta), and Wayne Cameron as a Managing Director in Mississauga. The trading leadership is organized by geographic region: Anthony Rhynold directs Asia Trading (since March 2021), Luis Pineiro directs Latin American Trading, Karim Grana directs International Sales, and Stewart Hanna heads the Australian meat trading operations. Dominick Melrose manages Domestic Logistics and meat trading in Australia. This is a substantially larger and more structured leadership team than what the current website conveys.
- SGF's product portfolio spans ten distinct categories across proteins, produce, and processed goods. Proteins include pork (a core strength — SGF exhibits under the Canada Pork banner at Anuga), beef (including the Argyle grassfed line), chicken, turkey, lamb, goat, and seafood. The produce division offers a complete line of Individually Quick Frozen (IQF) fruits and vegetables, sourced from various countries worldwide, available for customers who use them as ingredients or blends in further-processed products or in food service applications — and notably, many of their IQF products are available as Organic. Juice concentrates and purees represent another distinct category. The newest addition is frozen potato products — French fries (available in straight cut, crinkle cut, and steak cut in all standard sizes), hash brown patties, and potato wedges. Pulses (lentils, chickpeas, dry peas) are referenced in some mockup content but could not be independently verified as a current traded category through public sources.
- SGF operates through a network of international offices spanning multiple continents. Four offices are well-documented through multiple sources: the Mississauga, Ontario headquarters (2333 North Sheridan Way, Suite 100); an Atlanta, Georgia USA office (5901 Peachtree Dunwoody Road, Suite B225, also referenced at 7000 Central Parkway); and two Australian offices — North Sydney (Level 2, 97 Pacific Highway) and Maroochydore, Queensland (Kon-Tiki Business Centre, 55 Plaza Parade). Three additional offices — Manila (Philippines), Mexico City (Mexico), and Bristol (England) — are reported by a single third-party aggregator (aiHit) whose data was last updated approximately 3.5 years ago; these should be verified with the client as current before being listed on the new website. The Australian expansion began in 2019 when Sure Good Foods opened its first Australian office in Sydney, just two months after establishing Sure Good Foods USA in Atlanta. The company's stated intent was to build a 'full-service Australian meat trading organisation' focused on the large-volume commodity export meat trade out of Australia, including frozen manufacturing beef into North America and Asian markets.
- SGF's geographic market coverage is extensive and segmented by region. In Asia, the company actively sells into Japan, China, Hong Kong, Korea, Taiwan, Vietnam, Philippines, Singapore, and Malaysia. In North America, they trade throughout Canada, the United States, and Mexico. In the Caribbean and Latin America, they sell into Chile, Colombia, Peru, Puerto Rico, Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, and Costa Rica. Their customer base extends to Western and Eastern Europe. The international offices have expanded coverage into Central Asia, Southeast Asia, the Oceania region, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe. Import/export records from Panjiva show 3,585 bills of lading filed between August 2017 and March 2026, with 654 records in the past year alone and 59 in the most recent 30 days — confirming active, high-frequency trade operations.
- The November 2023 commercial partnership with Argyle Foods Group represents SGF's most significant strategic move into premium, certification-backed supply chains. Argyle is a vertically integrated Australian beef producer led by CEOs Lachlan and Bryce Graham, offering certified grassfed, GMO-free, antibiotic-free, HGP-free (hormone growth promotant-free), and never-confined beef. Under the partnership, SGF brings its experienced global sales team to support the growth of Argyle's bespoke grass and grain-fed brands, building on Argyle's existing footprint across China, Asia, and the US. Troy Warren stated the partnership would 'further integrate with the supply chain and enable customers to engage with the product prior to sales,' while Lachlan Graham said they were 'ready to expand our pastoral program with other likeminded producers.' This partnership signals SGF's evolution from a pure commodity trader toward premium branded protein supply chains — a differentiator that is not mentioned anywhere on the current website.
- SGF holds active memberships in four North American export trade associations: Canada Pork International (CPI), the Canadian Meat Council (CMC), the United States Meat Export Federation (USMEF), and the United States Poultry and Egg Export Council (USAPEEC). These memberships are significant because they signal institutional credibility to procurement buyers and provide access to trade delegations, market intelligence, and promotional programs in export markets. SGF exhibited at Anuga 2025 in Cologne, Germany — one of the world's largest food and beverage trade fairs with over 8,000 exhibitors from 110 countries — in Hall 06.1, Booth E050, represented at the Canada Pork booth. The Anuga exhibitor profile lists SGF's product categories as frozen meat (unprocessed and processed), pork, other meat products, meat preserves, seasoning mixtures, and frozen meat-based ready-meals, with distribution channels spanning manufacturer's brand products, food service and catering, food processing industry, and private label products for wholesale and retail trade.
- SGF's business model is that of a pure-play international food commodity trading intermediary. The company does not own production facilities, farms, processing plants, or retail brands. It earns margin on three core services: (1) Sourcing — traders with long-standing vendor-partner relationships identify the best international source of supply for each client's specific requirements, developing custom product solutions; (2) Logistics — an expert team provides around-the-clock monitoring and tracking of product shipments through logistics providers located around the globe, with the capability to distribute to any location worldwide; (3) Documentation and regulatory compliance — SGF handles customs procedures, regulatory health certification, and ensures timely and accurate pickup and delivery of all products and accompanying documentation. This trading model, rather than a manufacturing model, is a critical distinction for website content because the value proposition is expertise, network, and execution reliability — not factory capacity or proprietary production.
- No food safety certifications (GFSI-benchmarked standards such as SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000, or IFS; religious certifications such as Halal or Kosher; or organic certifications) are surfaced anywhere on SGF's website, LinkedIn profiles, industry directory listings, Anuga exhibitor profile, or any indexed web content. This absence is consistent across all sources reviewed. For a trading intermediary (as opposed to a manufacturer or processor), the company may rely on the certifications held by its supply chain partners rather than holding facility-based certifications itself, since SGF does not operate production or processing facilities. However, the complete absence of any certification language — even a statement about sourcing from certified suppliers — is a gap that procurement buyers at major retailers and foodservice distributors will notice, as GFSI certification verification is increasingly a standard due diligence step.
- The 80,000 square foot facility claim that appears on the current mockup website could not be verified through any public source. No warehouse, distribution center, or facility square footage is documented in SGF's Panjiva trade records, Dun and Bradstreet profile, LinkedIn page, Anuga exhibitor listing, industry directories, or any other indexed content. Given SGF's business model as a trading intermediary that does not own production or processing facilities, this claim should be verified directly with the client before being used in website copy. If SGF does operate or lease a warehouse or consolidation facility, it would be a meaningful proof point for the website — but it must be verified as factual.
- SGF's US import activity, documented through 3,585 bills of lading on ImportInfo.com spanning August 2017 to March 2026, reveals the operational scale and product mix in detail. The company primarily imports frozen beef products (frozen boneless beef topside, frozen boneless beef brisket in carton quantities of 1,000-1,100 per shipment), with sourcing from Paraguay, Brazil (Port of Santos), Colombia (Cartagena), New Zealand (Tauranga), and Canada (Vancouver, Halifax). The top US ports of entry are Seattle, Washington (1,496 records), Newark, New Jersey (880 records), Los Angeles/Long Beach, California (363 combined records), and Tacoma, Washington (211 records). Shipments are containerized, temperature-controlled at -18C, averaging approximately 25,000+ kilograms per container. Sure Good Foods USA Inc. is listed as an associated entity with 1,358 records, and JBS S.A. appears as a trading partner with 28,224 records — indicating SGF trades with some of the world's largest meat processors.
- SGF's social media presence is limited to LinkedIn, where the main Sure Good Foods Limited page had approximately 6,300+ followers as of early 2026 and the Sure Good Foods Australia page had approximately 67 followers at the same point in time. No verified presence was found on Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, or YouTube. The LinkedIn page serves as the only content distribution channel with any active audience. Individual team members maintain LinkedIn profiles that reference SGF, including traders, directors, and logistics coordinators, but there is no coordinated content marketing strategy visible across any platform.
- SGF's digital presence significantly understates the company's actual operational scale and history. The company has been operating for 25+ years, handles 46,000+ metric tonnes annually, maintains seven offices across four continents, employs approximately 60-99 people, serves 3,200+ B2B customers in 70+ countries through 60+ traders, has a partnership with a vertically integrated Australian beef producer, holds four major industry association memberships, and exhibits at the world's largest food trade fairs. Yet the website is a brochure-grade Wix build with a 2021 copyright date, no content depth, no certification language, no partner logos, no client testimonials, no geographic reach visualization, and no content marketing footprint. The gap between SGF's operational reality and its digital presentation is the core opportunity for the website redesign — every fact in this investigation is currently invisible online and belongs on the new site.
- The Australian division represents SGF's most significant international expansion, launched in 2019 with offices in North Sydney and later Maroochydore, Queensland. The operation was initially led by Stewart Hanna (previously a senior trader and North America sales manager at Sanger Australia) and later by Michael Niblett as Managing Director. The Australian offices focus on the large-volume commodity export meat trade out of Australia, including frozen manufacturing beef into North America and markets across Asia. The 2023 Argyle Foods Group partnership deepened the Australian connection, adding premium branded beef programs to SGF's commodity trading portfolio. Sure Good Foods Australia Pty Ltd is registered as a separate entity and maintains its own LinkedIn page and Panjiva trade records, with the North Sydney office listed at Level 2, 97 Pacific Highway.
- The Anuga 2025 exhibitor profile reveals product capabilities beyond what SGF's own website communicates. The trade fair listing categorizes SGF's offerings as: frozen meat (unprocessed and processed), frozen meat-based ready-meals, pork (unprocessed meat), other meat products, meat preserves, and seasoning mixtures and marinades for butchers. The distribution channel categorization is equally revealing: manufacturer's brand products for food wholesale and retail, food service and catering products, food processing industry products, and private label products for wholesale and retail trade. The private label capability is particularly noteworthy — SGF appears to facilitate private label programs for retail and wholesale clients — a service not mentioned on their current website.
- Based on the available evidence, SGF's supply chain appears to operate through a structure resembling a regional office network, with each office staffed by traders who specialize in specific geographic markets: Anthony Rhynold covers Asia from the Mississauga HQ, Luis Pineiro covers Latin America, Jamie Martel leads North American trading from Atlanta, Stewart Hanna and Dominick Melrose cover Australian exports, and Karim Grana handles international sales. These traders maintain long-standing relationships with vendor partners (producers, processors, and other trading companies) and develop custom product solutions for buyers. The company's Panjiva records show trading relationships with major global processors including JBS S.A. The logistics team coordinates temperature-controlled containerized shipments — typically at -18C for frozen products — through major international ports, handling customs documentation and regulatory health certification for each destination market.

## Concepts & Entities

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Pure-Play Commodity Trader | A firm that buys and sells physical food commodities across international borders without owning production, processing, or farming infrastructure. SGF earns margin on sourcing expertise, logistics coordination, documentation handling, and market access rather than on manufacturing. This is the fundamental distinction that shapes every aspect of the website: the value proposition is trust, network, and execution — not factory capacity. |
| IQF (Individually Quick Frozen) | A freezing method where individual pieces of food (fruit, vegetables, potato products) are frozen separately rather than in a block, preserving texture, color, and nutritional value. SGF trades a complete line of IQF fruits and vegetables for customers using them as ingredients in further-processed products or in food service applications. |
| HGP-Free (Hormone Growth Promotant-Free) | A product certification indicating beef was raised without synthetic growth hormones. A key requirement for EU market access and premium retail buyers. Central to the Argyle Foods Group partnership, alongside grassfed, GMO-free, antibiotic-free, and never-confined designations. |
| Bills of Lading (BOL) | Legal documents issued by carriers to shippers detailing the type, quantity, and destination of goods being transported. SGF has 3,585 BOLs on file in US trade databases from August 2017 to March 2026 — a verifiable measure of trade volume and operational activity. |
| Anuga | The world's largest food and beverage trade fair, held biennially in Cologne, Germany. In 2025 it hosted 8,000+ exhibitors from 110 countries. SGF exhibited in Hall 06.1, Booth E050 under the Canada Pork banner, positioning itself alongside major Canadian meat exporters. |
| Canada Pork International (CPI) | The export trade promotion agency for the Canadian pork industry, representing producers and processors in international markets. SGF's CPI membership and co-exhibition at Anuga under the Canada Pork banner signals deep integration with the Canadian pork export ecosystem. |
| Private Label Trading | Facilitating the sale of food products under a retailer's or wholesaler's own brand rather than the producer's brand. SGF's Anuga profile lists private label products for wholesale and retail trade as a distribution channel — a capability not surfaced on their current website. |
| GFSI (Global Food Safety Initiative) | An industry body that benchmarks food safety management standards (SQF, BRCGS, FSSC 22000, IFS). Many major retailers and foodservice distributors require GFSI certification from suppliers. SGF does not surface any GFSI or food safety certifications online, which may reflect reliance on supply chain partners' certifications rather than direct facility certification. |

## Tensions & Tradeoffs

- The 80,000 square foot facility claim on the current mockup cannot be verified through any public source, and SGF's business model as a trading intermediary (with no manufacturing) raises the question of whether this refers to office space, leased warehouse capacity, or partner facilities — the website content must accurately represent what the company actually operates.
- Three conflicting annual volume figures exist across sources: SGF's current website describes handling 'over 1 million lbs' annually; the 2023 Beef Central article on the Argyle partnership cites 46,000 metric tonnes (approximately 101 million lbs); and the earlier 2019 Beef Central article on the Australian expansion states SGF 'handles the shipment of about 200,000 tonnes' annually — a four-fold discrepancy even between the two trade publication figures, and a two-orders-of-magnitude gap between the website claim and the lower trade publication figure. The correct current figure should be confirmed directly with the client before being used in website copy.
- The absence of food safety certifications may be entirely appropriate for a trading intermediary that does not operate processing facilities, but procurement buyers increasingly expect some form of certification language from all supply chain participants — the website needs to address this either by surfacing partner certifications or by explaining SGF's quality assurance approach within the trading model.
- Employee and customer counts vary across sources: LinkedIn shows ~50-200 employees, RocketReach cites 84, third-party directories say 60-99, and some profiles cite 3,200+ customers while older data shows 400+ customers managed by 35 traders — the current scale should be confirmed with the client before publishing on the new website.
- The Anuga exhibitor profile lists product categories and distribution channels (including private label and food service) that are not mentioned on SGF's own website — there may be capabilities or services the company offers that are simply not being communicated online, or the Anuga listing may overstate the company's actual direct involvement in those channels.

## Open Questions

- Does SGF operate or lease any warehouse, cold storage, or distribution facility — and if so, what is the actual square footage? The 80K sqft claim on the mockup needs verification before inclusion in website copy.
- Does SGF hold or facilitate any food safety certifications (GFSI, HACCP, Halal, Kosher, organic) either directly or through formal partnerships with certified suppliers — or does the company rely entirely on its supply chain partners' certifications?
- What is the current status and scale of the Argyle Foods Group partnership since the November 2023 announcement — is it generating meaningful volume, and should Argyle's branded programs be featured prominently on the new website?
- Does SGF have a Dubai or UAE office? The prior research mentioned Dubai operations, but no public source confirms a physical office or registered entity in the UAE — this should be verified with the client.
- Is SGF actively trading in pulses (lentils, chickpeas, dry peas)? This category appeared in some prior mockup content but could not be verified through any public source.
- What are the accurate, current figures for employee count (sources range from 60 to 99 to 200), annual tonnage (46,000t from 2023 press vs. varying website claims), and customer count (400+ in 2019 Beef Central article vs. 3,200+ in recent profiles)?
- Does SGF directly offer private label programs for retail and wholesale clients, as suggested by the Anuga exhibitor profile, or does it facilitate private label through its trading partners?
- Who is Joe Wilkinson and what is his current relationship with SGF? He founded Wilkinson Foods International in 2000 and is listed as 'Owner' on LinkedIn, but does not appear in any current leadership listing — did Forgan and McCague acquire the business or did Wilkinson retire?
- Has SGF participated in other trade shows besides Anuga (e.g., SIAL Paris, Gulfood Dubai, IFFA Frankfurt, CIMIE) — trade show presence belongs on the website's credibility page?
